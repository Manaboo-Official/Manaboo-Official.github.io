とりビーですかね (tori bee desu ka ne)
“Kick it off with a beer?” A super-casual contraction of とりあえずビール (“beer for now”) often heard at izakaya or casual dinners when everyone’s ready to place the first drink order.

ブサカワ (Busakawa)
a fun bit of Japanese slang that literally mashes together ブサイク (busaiku, "ugly") and カワイイ (kawaii, "cute"). Think of something (often a pet, mascot, stuffed toy, or even a meme-y celebrity) that's objectively homely yet somehow irresistibly charming.

いただきます (itadakimasu)
"I humbly receive." — A short, polite phrase said right before you start eating or drinking to show gratitude for the food, the people who prepared it, and the life that was taken to provide it.
